# Giving Feedback Playbook

> Ask Claude: "Help me give feedback to [name] about [situation]"

## The SBI Framework

**Situation** - When and where
**Behavior** - What you observed (facts, not interpretation)
**Impact** - The effect on you, others, or the work

### Positive Feedback Example

"In yesterday's stakeholder meeting (Situation), when you pushed back on the unrealistic timeline with specific data about past projects (Behavior), it helped us land on a more achievable plan and built credibility with the team (Impact)."

### Constructive Feedback Example

"In our sprint planning today (Situation), when you committed to deliverables without checking with engineering first (Behavior), it created confusion about what we can actually deliver and put me in a tough spot with the team (Impact)."

## Preparing Feedback

### Before You Give Feedback

- [ ] Is this mine to give? (Am I the right person?)
- [ ] Is it timely? (Ideally within days, not weeks)
- [ ] Is it specific? (Not "you always..." or "you never...")
- [ ] Is it actionable? (Can they do something with it?)
- [ ] What's my intent? (To help them grow, not to vent)

### Questions to Ask Yourself

1. **What did I observe?** (Stick to facts)
2. **What was the impact?** (On work, team, relationships)
3. **What do I want to happen differently?**
4. **What context might I be missing?**

## Delivering Feedback

### Setting

- Private for constructive feedback
- Timely (not saved up for months)
- When you're both calm
- In person or video when possible

### Structure

1. **Ask permission** (optional, but helpful)
   "Can I share some feedback on the presentation?"

2. **State the situation**
   "In the client meeting yesterday..."

3. **Describe the behavior**
   "...you interrupted several times when the client was explaining their concerns..."

4. **Explain the impact**
   "...I noticed the client seemed to withdraw, and we didn't get the full picture of their needs."

5. **Invite their perspective**
   "I wanted to share that with you. What's your take?"

6. **Discuss and agree on next steps**
   "What do you think would help in future meetings?"

## Common Feedback Scenarios

### Missed Deadline

**Don't:** "You're always late on deliverables."

**Do:** "The analytics report was due Monday and came in Wednesday (Situation/Behavior). The team couldn't prepare for Thursday's meeting, and we looked unprepared to leadership (Impact). What happened, and how can we prevent this?"

### Quality Issues

**Don't:** "Your work has been sloppy lately."

**Do:** "In the last two PRDs (Situation), I found several incomplete sections and data that didn't match our metrics dashboard (Behavior). I spent extra time validating before I could share with stakeholders (Impact). Let's talk about what's happening."

### Communication Style

**Don't:** "You come across as aggressive."

**Do:** "In the design review (Situation), when you said 'that's a terrible idea' about Sarah's proposal (Behavior), I noticed she stopped contributing for the rest of the meeting (Impact). What was behind that reaction?"

### Great Work

**Don't:** "Good job!" (Too vague)

**Do:** "The competitive analysis you put together (Situation) had exactly the depth the exec team needed, especially the pricing comparison table (Behavior). It directly influenced our pricing strategy decision (Impact). Well done."

## Receiving Feedback

### In the Moment

1. **Listen without defending**
2. **Ask clarifying questions** - "Can you give me a specific example?"
3. **Thank them** - It took courage to share
4. **Take time to process** - "I appreciate this. Let me think about it."

### After

- Reflect honestly on what's true
- Decide what to do with it
- Follow up if you committed to changes

## Feedback Timing

### Give Feedback Soon When:
- Safety or significant impact
- Pattern is forming
- They'd want to know immediately

### Wait for 1:1 When:
- Sensitive topic
- Needs more discussion
- Might need their context first

### Skip It When:
- One-time minor thing
- They already know
- Not yours to give
- Would cause more harm than good

## Making Feedback a Habit

### In 1:1s

- Regular prompt: "What's one thing I could do differently?"
- After key moments: "How did that meeting go from your perspective?"
- Ongoing: Mix positive and constructive

### Team Culture

- Normalize feedback as gift, not criticism
- Model receiving feedback well
- Create psychological safety
- Separate feedback from performance ratings

## Warning Signs

**Your feedback might not land if:**
- You're angry or frustrated
- It's about personality, not behavior
- You're giving it to multiple people at once
- You're guessing at motives
- There's no specific example
- You don't have a relationship foundation
