# Difficult Conversations Playbook

> Ask Claude: "Help me prepare for a difficult conversation about [topic]"

## Before the Conversation

### Clarify Your Intent

**What happened?** (Observable facts only, no interpretation)


**What's the impact?** (On you, them, the team, the work)


**What do you want to achieve?**


**What's your contribution to the situation?**


### Check Your Mindset

- [ ] I'm coming from curiosity, not judgment
- [ ] I'm open to being wrong
- [ ] I care about this person and the relationship
- [ ] I've separated facts from my story about the facts

### Prepare Key Points

**Opening statement:** (Factual, specific, non-judgmental)

-

**What I need to express:**


## Conversation Structure

### 1. Open (Set the tone)

"I wanted to talk about [specific situation]. This is important to me because [reason]. I'd like to understand your perspective and share mine, and hopefully we can figure out a path forward together."

### 2. Share Your Perspective

- State facts: "I noticed that..."
- Share impact: "The impact was..."
- Own your interpretation: "The story I'm telling myself is..."
- Invite their view: "But I realize I might be missing something. What's your take?"

### 3. Listen to Understand

- Ask clarifying questions
- Reflect back what you hear
- Look for what's valid in their view
- Don't defend, just understand

### 4. Problem-Solve Together

- "What do you think we should do?"
- "What would help?"
- "What can I do differently?"
- "What agreements should we make?"

### 5. Close with Clarity

- Summarize agreements
- Set follow-up if needed
- Express appreciation for the conversation

## Common Scenarios

### Performance Issues

**Frame as:** Gap between expectations and current performance

**Key points:**
- Be specific about the gap
- Ask what's getting in the way
- Agree on what success looks like
- Set clear timeline and check-ins

### Interpersonal Conflict

**Frame as:** Working relationship that needs attention

**Key points:**
- Focus on behaviors, not personality
- Acknowledge your part
- Look for underlying interests
- Find common ground

### Delivering Bad News

**Frame as:** Honest, direct, compassionate

**Key points:**
- Lead with the news (don't bury it)
- Be clear and direct
- Acknowledge the impact
- Provide support/resources

### Pushback on Decisions

**Frame as:** Legitimate disagreement worth understanding

**Key points:**
- Listen fully first
- Acknowledge valid concerns
- Explain reasoning (not just the decision)
- Be clear about what's negotiable vs. not

## After the Conversation

- [ ] Document key agreements
- [ ] Follow up on commitments
- [ ] Check in on the relationship
- [ ] Reflect: What did I learn?

## Red Flags to Watch

In yourself:
- Wanting to "win"
- Rehearsing your response while they talk
- Dismissing their perspective
- Feeling attacked and getting defensive

In the conversation:
- They're shutting down
- It's escalating
- You're going in circles
- New information changes things

**If things go sideways:** "I think we both need some time. Can we pause and come back to this tomorrow?"
